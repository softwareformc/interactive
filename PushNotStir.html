Makes about four servings

INGREDIENTS


1 tablespoon finely chopped ginger
1 tablespoon finely chopped garlic
1 to 1 ¼ lb plain white tofu cubed into ¾” pieces
salt
2 teaspoons potato flour mixed with 2 tbsp cold water
4 baby leeks or spring onions, green parts only
4 tablespoons cooking oil
2 ½ tablespoons sichuan chili bean paste
1 tablespoons fermented black beans, rinsed and drained
2 teaspoons ground red chillies (optional)
½ cup vegetarian stock or water
¼ teaspoons ground white pepper
¼-½ teaspoons ground roasted Sichuan Pepper



A note on chopping your spring onions: If you feel like getting fancy cut them in a horse ear shape, like so: (illustration here)

Notes Use at least a 14” wide diameter wok – a normal flat pan works as well. For this dish, I prefer a softer tofu over a firmer one. But really anything you can find works. A word of caution: only add ground red chilies if you want it very spicy.
